The DCKC progression system
Levels reflect your experience and help leaders make good decisions about who is ready for which trips. They are not badges or rewards — they are a shared language for safety and trust within the club.
Before membership
🌱
Aspirant
New to DCKC. You have three trial sessions to experience the club before committing to full membership. After your third session membership is required to continue paddling with us.
Zone 1 — Club progression
Level 1
🐸
Frog
New full member · finding your feet on the water

Frogs have just taken the big leap of starting kayaking or canoeing for the first time, either on their own or with a friend, and may not have done a capsize drill before with a spray-deck on.

You can ask someone more experienced to show you a capsize at any Tuesday evening event — it's often something left to the end of the evening so you can get straight out to dry off. At which point you turn from a frog... into a duck!

🌊 Sea
Flat calm, sheltered water only. Still finding stability in the boat.
🟢 River
Flat water and very gentle Grade 1 only.
What you can join
Tuesday Evening sessions — Loch Lomond and All Away
Pinkston 1 pump sessions
Pool and skills sessions
Second Saturday paddles — Sea Grade A, with leader approval
Advancing to Duck
There are no fixed criteria — advancement is based on the judgement of experienced members who have paddled with you.
👥
One witness at Dolphin level or above corroborates your readiness. A Paddling Admin sets your approval ceiling accordingly.
Level 2
🦆
Duck
Developing paddler · comfortable on sheltered water

Ducks have carried out a capsize drill, with a spray-deck on, in a kayak — either in a swimming pool or outside. You can ask someone more experienced to show you this at any Tuesday evening meet.

Note: having done a capsize on a sit-on-top does not count unfortunately.

🌊 Sea
Sheltered coastal water and light chop. Comfortable in Force 2–3. Beginning to read wind and conditions.
🟢 River
Grade 1–2. Straightforward moving water with simple currents.
What you can join
Everything at Frog level
Sea Grade A and B trips, with leader approval
Pinkston 1 and 2 pump sessions
River trips up to Grade 2/3, with leader approval
Training and qualification courses
Advancing to Otter
Again, no fixed criteria. The question leaders ask is whether you paddle with good awareness and judgement, and whether you're ready for more committing water.
👥
Two witnesses at Paddlesport Leader level or above must corroborate your readiness. A Paddling Admin sets your new approval ceiling.
Level 3
🦦
Otter
Experienced paddler · ready for open water and moving water
Your level

Otters can reliably perform a rescue successfully while on the water — using a heel hook and not swimming to the shore or bank — and have also been rescued successfully.

Note: if you have performed a rescue in a kayak this does not make you an Otter in other craft. It would not make you an Otter in a canoe or on a SUP.

🌊 Sea
Comfortable in waves and Force 4 winds. Can maintain boat control and perform rescues in confused sea conditions.
🟢 River
Confident on Grade 3. Reads water well and can execute rescues on moving water.
What you can join
Everything at Duck level
Sea Grade B and C trips, with leader approval
Pinkston 1, 2 and 3 pump sessions
River trips up to Grade 3/4, with leader approval
Multi-day expeditions and overnight trips
Advancing to Dolphin
Dolphin is a significant step — it recognises a paddler who is well-rounded, has broad experience, and is approaching the level where leadership becomes possible.
👥
Two witnesses at Sea Kayak Leader or Whitewater Kayak Leader level or above. A Paddling Admin sets your new approval ceiling.
Level 4
🐬
Dolphin
Advanced paddler · broad experience, emerging leader

Dolphins are leaders. Amongst other things they can perform rescues on others, reliably do a self rescue, and are authorised by DCKC to run trips.

Dolphin is the highest level within the club's internal progression — an accomplished paddler with wide experience across disciplines and conditions, trusted to take responsibility for others on the water.

🌊 Sea
Confident in Force 4+ and exposed conditions. Reliable roll. Can lead and perform rescues on others in demanding sea states.
🟢 River
Confident on Grade 4. Reliable roll. Reads complex water and supports others through technical sections.
What you can join
All club trips — full access, subject to leader approval
Grade C sea expeditions
Grade 4 and above river trips
Can act as assistant leader on appropriate trips
Can corroborate Frog → Duck advancement as a single witness
The next step
Advancement beyond Dolphin moves into British Canoeing's formal qualification pathway. This is externally assessed and recognised nationally.
🏅
Paddlesport Leader, Sea Kayak Leader, or Whitewater Kayak Leader — assessed externally through British Canoeing. Speak to a Paddling Admin about supported pathways.
Zone 2 — British Canoeing qualifications
Level 5
🦭
Selkie
Qualified leader · nationally recognised
British Canoeing

Selkie encompasses all British Canoeing leadership qualifications held by DCKC members. Within this level there is a spectrum — from Paddlesport Leader through to Advanced Sea Kayak Leader — and a Selkie's approval ceiling reflects their specific qualifications.

Selkie members are the leaders of the club. They create trips, approve member requests, mentor the progression of others, and carry responsibility for safety on the water.

Qualifications within Selkie
Paddlesport Leader — general leadership on sheltered water
Sea Kayak Leader (SKL) — leads sea trips to Grade B/C
Whitewater Kayak Leader (WWKL) — leads river trips to Grade 4+
Advanced SKL — leads advanced sea expeditions
Advanced WWKL — leads advanced whitewater
Responsibilities
Selkie members can create and lead club events, approve or decline member join requests, corroborate progression for all lower levels, and act as witnesses for Duck → Otter and Otter → Dolphin advancement.
📋
BC qualifications are verified by a Paddling Admin and recorded against your profile. Speak to an admin to update your record after gaining a new qualification.
Questions about your level or progression? Speak to a Paddling Admin or any Selkie member.
