Paddled Kentallen · Sep 2023 · with Iain M.
📌 Leader notes — private
Strong paddler, good group awareness. Slightly hesitant in tidal conditions last time out. Worth watching in the narrows.
Question 1
How many years have you been paddling?
About 5 years, mostly sea kayaking around the west coast of Scotland. Started with the club 3 years ago.
Question 2
Where have your top paddles of all time been?
Skye circumnavigation last summer was a highlight. Also loved the Garvellachs trip with DCKC in 2023. Did some paddling in Norway a few years back — fjord touring, fairly sheltered but stunning.
Question 3
Where have you encountered your toughest conditions and what happened?
Got caught out in a Force 5 squall coming around the Mull of Kintyre. Conditions built faster than forecast. We rafted up, waited it out in a sheltered bay. Iain was leading — good learning experience.
Question 4
How confident do you feel judging Beaufort scale and sea state?
⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ Fairly confident
Question 5
What do you think makes a good paddling group?
Clear communication before and during the trip. Everyone knowing their role. A leader who briefs well and checks in with the group. Not pushing on when conditions are against you.
Question 6
Is there anything the leader should know about you before the trip?
I take blood pressure medication — nothing that affects paddling but it's in my ICE card. I'm a strong swimmer if that's relevant.
Question 7
What is your favourite group size to paddle with?
6-8 people. Small enough to move efficiently but enough people to share the experience with.
